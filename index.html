About Me

I am a mechanical engineer and acoustics researcher focused on transcranial focused ultrasound, acoustic holography, and experimental validation of complex wave fields. My work combines numerical modeling (k-Wave, COMSOL, mSOUND) with 3D printing and hydrophone-based measurements.

Research Highlights

  • Design and validation of multiplex acoustic holographic lenses for transcranial neuromodulation.
  • Automated 3D hydrophone scanning system for mapping ultrasound fields.
  • Elastic–acoustic mode conversion modeling at multilayer interfaces.

Selected Projects

Multiplex Acoustic Holographic Lenses for Transcranial FUS

Designed elastic holographic lenses using CT-derived skull models and elastic–acoustic angular spectrum simulations. Implemented thickness optimization, mode conversion corrections, and validated focus profiles with a 3D hydrophone scanning setup.

3D Hydrophone Scanning System

Developed an automated measurement platform integrating a function generator, Tektronix oscilloscope, and multi-axis positioning system in MATLAB. Enabled point-by-point acquisition and FFT-based pressure extraction for full 3D field mapping.

Non-Pneumatic Tire (NPT) Thermal & Structural Optimization

Investigated a skewed hexagonal honeycomb NPT geometry for enhanced convective brake cooling. Performed CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent and FEA-based structural analysis to identify geometries that balance heat rejection and mechanical integrity.

ABET Outcome-Level Assessment Dashboard

Built data-driven dashboards to track course and program outcome attainment (C3-1 to C3-7), including rubric-based metrics, confidence intervals, and visual summaries to support accreditation decisions.
