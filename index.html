About Me

I am a mechanical engineer and acoustics researcher focused on transcranial focused ultrasound, acoustic holography, and experimental validation of complex wave fields. My work combines numerical modeling (k-Wave, COMSOL, mSOUND) with 3D printing and hydrophone-based measurements.

Research Highlights

Selected Projects

Acoustic Holography & Transcranial Ultrasound

Experimental and computational investigation of elastic and acoustic wave propagation in layered media for transcranial focused ultrasound, integrating simulation, fabrication, and 3D hydrophone-based validation.

  • Constructed simulation models using k-Wave and mSOUND to study wave propagation through skull and multilayer holographic lenses.
  • Applied optimization algorithms to synthesize arbitrary and volumetric target patterns in highly heterogeneous media.
  • Integrated COMSOL-derived stress tensors with the angular spectrum method to identify coupled modes in multilayer acoustic lenses.
  • Developed and validated patient-specific skull-conformal AHLs using neuroimaging data, numerical modeling, and hydrophone measurements.

Skull-Conformal Acoustic Holographic Lenses

Design and realization of patient-specific, skull-conformal acoustic holographic lenses to enhance transcranial therapeutic ultrasound by improving targeting accuracy and energy delivery through complex cranial geometries.

  • Translated CT/MRI-derived skull morphology into computational models for generating conformal hologram phase maps.
  • Implemented SLA 3D printing workflows to realize multiplex, multilayer holograms tailored to individual skull geometries.
  • Performed 3D hydrophone-based pressure field scans to validate focusing accuracy and mode-coupling behavior.
  • Co-inventor on a provisional USPTO patent for individualized wavefield vectoring in skull-conformal neuromodulation.

Non-Pneumatic Tire (NPT) with Skewed Hexagonal Honeycomb Structure

Developed and investigated a novel non-pneumatic tire (NPT) concept using skewed hexagonal honeycomb structures to reduce contact pressure and rolling resistance while enhancing brake cooling via forced convection.

  • Innovated conventional NPT designs by skewing boundary planar geometries to improve airflow and cooling.
  • Used FEA and CFD (ANSYS Fluent) to study the impact of skewness on structural response and airflow.
  • Computed aerodynamic parameters for rotating cases and modeled heat transfer over a brake rotor.
  • Identified an optimum skewness range balancing structural integrity and forced convection performance.

Publication: ASME IMECE Paper (2018)

Active Magnetic Bearing (AMB): Design, Analysis & Test Bench Fabrication

Designed and analyzed an active magnetic bearing system that uses electromagnetic levitation to support a rotor without mechanical contact, aiming to reduce wear, frictional losses, and enable ultra-high rotational speeds.

  • Designed a multi-pole electromagnetic stator structure with independently controlled poles for precision levitation.
  • Derived models for force–current relationships, magnetic stiffness, slew rate, and specific load capacity.
  • Simulated flux densities and force generation using finite element solvers, identifying saturation limits.
  • Built a benchtop prototype to experimentally measure force efficiency and validate analytical predictions.

Go-Kart Design & Fabrication

Designed and fabricated a custom go-kart, integrating mechanical design, material selection, and hands-on manufacturing for a student competition.

  • Created CAD models for chassis, steering, and drivetrain components with safety and stiffness constraints.
  • Performed structural and weight analysis to optimize the frame geometry.
  • Fabricated the chassis using welding and machining processes and integrated powertrain and braking systems.
  • Conducted test runs to evaluate performance, handling, and durability.
